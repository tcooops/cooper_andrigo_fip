About Craft Beer

Aka Frequently Asked Questions

What is ABV?

ABV stands for Alcohol by volume. The higher the ABV of a beer, the higher the alcohol content. ABV is the standard measurement used worldwide to assess the strength of a particular beer.The average alcohol content for craft beer is 5.9%. Some beers can be as low as 2% to as high as 10% or more. The percentage gets higher with heavier beers.

What is IBU?

IBU stands for International Bitterness Units. IBU is a scale that gauges a beer’s bitterness based on the hops added, and is typically the main deciding factor for someone looking to try a new craft beer. The IBU can be confusing as the higher the IBU doesn’t always mean the beer has a very bitter flavour.

How is craft beer made?

Beer is made from four basic ingredients: Barley (or an alternative grain such as rice), water, hops, and yeast. The sugars are extracted from the grains, at which point the yeast can convert the sugar to alcohol and CO2, creating our favourite beverage… beer! Brewing beer is very scientific (and also a bit of an art if you ask us!).

I’m new to craft beer… where do I start?

The best way to find out what you like is to try different beer styles with varying IBUs. Gray Ridge Brewing offers flights in house and at many of our restaurant partners so that you can find your favourite Gray Ridge beer.
