Egg Drop Soup

Enjoy this Egg Drop Soup with a cold Leghorn Lager! Perfect for the family and an instant crowd pleaser, our Egg Drop Soup only takes a few ingredients that you almost always have in the fridge and takes only 25 minutes- start to finish!

  • Prep: 10 minutes
  • Cook: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients
  • 3 lightly beaten large eggs
  • 4 cups chicken stock (can be homemade or store bought)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • About a 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 3 chopped green onions
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 3/4 cup sliced shiitake mushrooms
How to:
  1. Make a cornstarch slur. Measure out 1/2 cup of the stock and mix with the cornstarch until dissolved.
  2. Put stock, ginger, soy sauce, onions, mushrooms, and white pepper in pot and bring to boil.
  3. Stir in the corn starch slurry and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Stir in beaten eggs.. now there IS a method to this: Slowly pour in the beaten eggs while stirring slowly. The egg will spread out into ribbons!
  5. Garnish and serve: Garnish with a few more chopped green onions, serve immediately.

Chicken Fried Egg Rice

Enjoy this homemade chicken fried rice with a Free Run IPA! Who would have thought you could make chicken fried rice at home that tastes better than take out! We did! And we blame it all on the delicious. You can swap the chicken out with just about any protein you’d like- pork, shrimp, even tofu.

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons canola or vegetable oil
  • 3/4 to 1 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • diced, 1 1/2 cups frozen peas and diced carrots blend (straight from freezer is fine!)
  • 3 sliced green onions, 2 to 3 mined garlic cloves, 3 lightly, beaten large eggs, 4 cups cooked rice *
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons soy sauce and salt and pepper to taste
How to:
  1. In a large non-stick skillet or wok, add the oils and chicken, and cook over medium-high heat for about 3 to 5 minutes, ensuring all sides cook evenly.
  2. Remove chicken with a slotted spoon (allow oils and cooking juices from chicken to remain in skillet) and set aside.
  3. Add the peas, carrots, green onions, and cook for about 2 minutes, or until vegetables begin to soften. Stir every once in a while.
  4. Next, add the garlic and cook for 1 minute making sure not to burn the garlic. Push vegetables to one side of the skillet, add the eggs to the other side, and cook to scramble.
  5. Add the chicken back in, add the rice and evenly drizzle with soy sauce. Add salt and pepper, and stir to combine. Cook for about 2 minutes, or until chicken is reheated through.
  6. Note* For the rice, it’s best if it’s day-old, cold rice. You can cook the rice ahead of time and leave in the fridge overnight or cook the rice first and thinly layer it on a cookie sheet to cool in the freezer while you prep. 5 minute rice is good enough, but you can use any rice you wish.

Pork with Béarnaise Sauce

Béarnaise sauce is basically the reason we live. And let us share it with you! Bearnaise sauce takes some time to perfect, but once you nail it, it’s WORTH it. We’ve simplified it here and decided on an unconventional protein route- veal! You’ll be pleasantly surprised by the bright flavours in this dish. The only thing that makes it better? Pairing it with a Silkie Stout.

  • Prep: 20 minutes
  • Cook: 50 minutes
  • Serves4:
Ingredients
  • For the pork:
  • 2 tablespoons butter or margarine
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 whole pork tenderloins (about 1 pound per person)
  • 1/3 cup dry red wine
  • 1/3 cup water and some salt and pepper
  • For the Béarnaise sauce:
  • 1/4 cup white wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 2 chopped shallots
  • 1 sprig fresh tarragon
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup melted and cooled butter
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh tarragon and salt and pepper
How to:
  1. In large heavy skillet, heat butter and oil over medium heat.
  2. Sprinkle pork with salt and pepper. Add whole tenderloins; cook, turning occasionally, until browned on all sides.
  3. Add wine and water; cover and cook over medium-low heat for 35 to 45 minutes When pork is plated, drizzle a generous amount of Béarnaise sauce on top.
  4. While cooking the pork, time to make the béarnaise! In a saucepan, bring the vinegar and wine to a boil with the shallots and tarragon sprig. Season with pepper.
  5. Reduce until only about 3 tablespoons of liquid remains. Strain.
  6. In a bowl over a saucepan of simmering water, with a whisk, beat the vinegar reduction and egg yolks until thick and foamy. Remove from the double boiler.
  7. Off the heat, drizzle in the butter, whisking constantly. Add the chopped tarragon. Adjust the seasoning. If the sauce seems too thick, whisk in a little hot water.
  8. Keep at room temperature and just before serving, place back on the double boiler, whisking for just a few seconds, to warm it up. Drizzle and ENJOY!
